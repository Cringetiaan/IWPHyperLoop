The rails serve as the backbone of the Hyperloop system, providing a stable and efficient track for the passenger pods to travel on.
Enabled by magnetic levitation technology, they forgo conventional wheels in favor of having the pods levitating a short distance above them.
This eliminates friction from the equation, allowing for higher top speeds as well as smoother acceleration and deceleration.
This technology has been around for quite a while, with the Shanghai Maglev Train (SMT) being in commercial use since 2004.
Powered by German Transrapid technology, this train completes a 30km journey in just 8 minutes and 10 seconds!
